## 1. DEFINITIONS

**"AI Tools"** means the artificial intelligence software, language models, voice agents, and automated systems integrated into the Platform, including but not limited to chatbots, classification engines, and document extraction tools.

**"Client Data"** means all data uploaded, entered, or generated by the Client or the Client's tenants through use of the Platform, including tenant personal information, lease documents, financial records, maintenance requests, messages, media files, and call recordings.

**"Personal Information"** has the meaning ascribed to it in the Act Respecting the Protection of Personal Information in the Private Sector (Quebec), CQLR c P-39.1 ("Law 25").

**"Platform"** means the Quietly property management dashboard, AI communication tools, voice agent, and all related software and services hosted and maintained by the Provider.

**"Services"** means the services described in Section 2 of this Agreement.

**"Subprocessor"** means a third-party service provider engaged by Quietly to process Personal Information on behalf of the Client, as listed in Schedule A.

**"Tenant"** means any individual whose Personal Information is processed through the Platform in connection with the Client's property management activities.

## 2. SERVICES

### 2.1 Services Provided

The Provider shall provide the Client with:

(a) Access to the Platform, including the property management dashboard, AI-powered tenant communication tools (chatbot and voice agent), multi-channel message intake (Telegram, WhatsApp, SMS, Email, Phone), ticket management, lease management, expense tracking, reporting tools, and announcement broadcasting;

(b) Ongoing platform maintenance, including bug fixes, security patches, and feature updates at the Provider's sole discretion;

(c) Technical support via email or messaging during business hours (Monday to Friday, 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM Eastern Time), with best-effort response times as described in Schedule B.

### 2.2 Services NOT Provided

The Provider does not provide and shall not be responsible for:

(a) Property management services or property management advice of any kind;

(b) Rent collection, financial management, or accounting services;

(c) Tenant onboarding, tenant screening, or direct communication with Tenants on behalf of the Client;

(d) Vendor selection, vendor dispatch, or vendor supervision;

(e) Legal, tax, insurance, or regulatory compliance advice;

(f) Emergency response services, 911 dispatch, or emergency coordination;

(g) Guarantees regarding the accuracy, completeness, or appropriateness of any AI-generated content.

The Client acknowledges that the Provider is a technology company providing software tools, not a property management company, and that the Client retains full responsibility for all property management decisions.

## 5. TERM AND TERMINATION

**5.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue on a month-to-month basis until terminated by either party in accordance with this Section.

**5.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either party may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the other party.

**5.3 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other party:

(a) Commits a material breach of this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within fifteen (15) days of receiving written notice thereof;

(b) Becomes insolvent, files for bankruptcy, or makes an assignment for the benefit of creditors;

(c) Uses the Platform for any unlawful purpose.

**5.4 Effect of Termination.**

(a) Upon termination or expiration, the Provider shall, at the Client's request, provide a complete export of all Client Data in standard formats (CSV, PDF, and media files) within fourteen (14) days of receiving the request.

(b) The Provider shall permanently delete all Client Data from its servers within thirty (30) days after confirmed delivery of the data export, or within thirty (30) days after the end of the ninety (90) day post-termination export window if no export is requested.

(c) Sections 3 (AI Disclaimer), 6.7 (Breach Notification), 7 (Limitation of Liability), 8 (Indemnification), 9 (Intellectual Property), and 10 (Confidentiality) shall survive termination.

## 6. DATA PROCESSING

### Roles and Scope

**6.1** For the purposes of Law 25, the Client is the person carrying on an enterprise that collects and controls the Personal Information of Tenants. The Provider is a service provider (sous-traitant) that processes Personal Information solely on behalf of the Client and in accordance with this Agreement.

**6.2** The Provider shall process the following categories of Personal Information on behalf of the Client:

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Identity | Tenant name, phone number, email address, unit number, language preference |
| Lease Data | Rent amount, lease dates, dwelling description, lessees, lease document |
| Communications | Message transcripts (text), call recordings (audio), photos |
| Maintenance | Ticket descriptions, categories, urgency levels, resolution history |
| Financial | Property expenses, receipts, mortgage details (if entered by Client) |

**6.3** The Provider shall process Personal Information solely for the purpose of providing the Services described in Section 2 and for no other purpose. The Provider shall not sell, rent, or otherwise disclose Personal Information to any third party except as described in this Agreement.

### Security

**6.4** The Provider shall implement and maintain reasonable security measures to protect Personal Information, including:

(a) Encryption of data in transit using TLS;

(b) Hosting on a dedicated virtual private server with restricted access via SSH key-based authentication;

(c) Dashboard access protected by authentication;

(d) Database not exposed to the public internet;

(e) Regular data backups maintained by the Provider.

**6.5** The Provider shall ensure that access to Personal Information is limited to authorized personnel on a need-to-know basis.

### Subprocessors

**6.6** The Client acknowledges and agrees that the Provider may engage the Subprocessors listed in Schedule A to assist in providing the Services. The Provider shall notify the Client in writing at least thirty (30) days before adding or replacing a Subprocessor. If the Client objects to a new Subprocessor on reasonable grounds related to data protection, the Client may terminate this Agreement in accordance with Section 5.2.

### Breach Notification

**6.7** In the event of a confidentiality incident (as defined in Law 25) involving Personal Information, the Provider shall:

(a) Notify the Client in writing within forty-eight (48) hours of confirming the incident;

(b) Provide the Client with all information reasonably necessary for the Client to assess the incident and fulfill the Client's notification obligations under Law 25;

(c) Cooperate with the Client in investigating and remediating the incident.

The Client is responsible for notifying the Commission d'acces a l'information du Quebec (CAI) and affected individuals in accordance with the timelines and requirements of Law 25.

### Retention and Deletion

**6.8** The Provider shall retain Client Data for the duration of this Agreement. Upon termination, data retention and deletion shall be governed by Section 5.4.

**6.9** The Client may request deletion of specific Personal Information at any time. The Provider shall comply with such requests within thirty (30) days, except where retention is required by law.

### Data Location

**6.10** Client Data is primarily stored on servers located in Canada and/or the European Union (Hostinger). Certain Subprocessors may process Personal Information in the United States or European Union, as indicated in Schedule A. The Provider shall notify the Client in writing if the primary data storage location changes.

### Audit Right

**6.11** The Client may, no more than once per calendar year and upon thirty (30) days' written notice, request a written summary of the Provider's data protection measures and practices. The Provider shall respond to such requests within thirty (30) days.
