1. System Overview

Your property management system automatically handles tenant communication across five channels. When a tenant sends a message or calls, the AI assistant responds, creates a maintenance ticket, and notifies you via Telegram.

Here is how every interaction flows:

  1. Tenant reaches out via Telegram, WhatsApp, SMS, Email, or Phone
  2. AI identifies the tenant by their phone number, email, or Telegram account
  3. AI responds — asks clarifying questions and suggests self-help solutions
  4. A ticket is created on your dashboard with a full summary
  5. You receive a Telegram notification with the ticket details
  6. If urgent, you get dispatch buttons to contact a vendor immediately

The system runs 24/7. Tenants can reach out at any time and the AI will respond immediately. You only need to step in when you choose to.

5. Managing Tenants

Adding a tenant

  1. Go to Tenants in the sidebar
  2. Click "Add Tenant" (top right)
  3. Fill in the details: • Name: First Last
    • Phone: With country code, digits only (e.g. 15145551234)
    • Email: Tenant's email address
    • Property: Select from dropdown
    • Unit Number: e.g. 203, 4B
    • Language: English or French
    • Telegram ID: Leave blank — auto-captured when tenant messages the bot
  4. Click Save

Phone number rules:

• Canadian numbers MUST start with 1 (country code): 15145551234

• Digits only — no spaces, dashes, or brackets

• International numbers: include their country code (e.g. 21698047018)

• The system will warn you if the format is wrong

Telegram ID: You never need to enter this manually. When a tenant sends their first message to the Telegram bot, the system captures their ID automatically. You can verify this happened by checking the Telegram ID column on the Tenants page.

Editing a tenant

Click the pencil icon next to any tenant → update their details → Save.

Removing a tenant

Click Delete next to the tenant. This removes their profile — the bot will no longer respond to their messages. Past tickets and messages are preserved for your records.

Adding a vendor

  2. Click "Add Vendor"

Categories

Each vendor is assigned to a service category. When a ticket is dispatched, the system looks up vendors matching the ticket's category. Available categories:

  • Electrical
  • HVAC (heating, ventilation, air conditioning)
  • Appliance

  • Locksmith

Tip: Make sure you have at least one vendor for each common category (plumbing, electrical, HVAC, locksmith). Without a vendor on file, the dispatch buttons won't have anyone to contact.

8. Ticket Lifecycle

How tickets are created

A ticket is created automatically whenever a tenant reports an issue, asks a question, or makes a request. This happens on the very first message — even while the AI is still asking clarifying questions. You do not need to create tickets manually.

Ticket statuses

StatusMeaning
OpenActive ticket — conversation is ongoing or awaiting action
ResolvedTenant confirmed the issue is fixed
ClosedTicket has been closed (e.g. video suggestion helped)
Wait for ApprovalVendor dispatch pending your approval

Active vs. Closed view

On the Tickets page, active tickets (open, wait_for_approval) appear at the top. Resolved and closed tickets are hidden by default in a collapsible section at the bottom — click "Resolved / Closed" to expand.

Closing a ticket manually

Open a ticket → click Mark Resolved (top right). The bot stops responding to that conversation. If the tenant messages again about a new issue, a new ticket is created automatically.

Deleting a ticket

Open a ticket → click Delete → confirm. This permanently removes the ticket, all messages, and attached photos. This cannot be undone.

9. Responding to Tenants

Letting the bot handle it

By default, the AI bot handles all tenant conversations automatically. It asks clarifying questions, suggests self-help solutions, and creates tickets. You only need to intervene when you want to.

Pausing the bot & replying manually

  1. Open a ticket and click Bot OFF (top right)The button turns amber to show the bot is paused.
  2. A text input appears at the bottom of the conversationType your message and press Enter or click Send.
  3. Your message is sent directly to the tenantIt goes via whatever channel they used (Telegram, WhatsApp, SMS, or Email).
  4. Bot auto-resumes when you leave the ticket pageYou don't need to manually turn it back on. Closing the tab or navigating away does it automatically.

Auto-resume: The bot automatically resumes when you leave the ticket page or close the browser tab. This ensures only one ticket is paused at a time and you can't accidentally leave the bot off permanently.

Note: You can only send manual messages on open tickets. If a ticket is resolved or closed, the input field will not appear.

10. Vendor Dispatch

How it works

When a tenant confirms an urgent issue (e.g. confirmed flooding, no heat in winter), you receive a Telegram notification with two buttons:

ButtonWhat It Does
Auto DispatchSystem looks up the best vendor for the category, drafts a message using AI, shows you a preview, and asks you to confirm before sending
Show ContractorsShows you the vendor list for that category so you can choose manually

Auto Dispatch flow

  1. You tap "Auto Dispatch" on the Telegram notification
  2. System looks up vendors matching the ticket category
  3. AI drafts a vendor message with the issue details
  4. You see a preview with Confirm / Cancel buttons
  5. You confirm → vendor receives an email with the issue details

Important: The system never dispatches a vendor without your explicit confirmation. You always see a preview and must tap Confirm.

11. Notifications

What you receive

  • Every new ticket — category, tenant name, unit, property, and summary
  • Urgent tickets — same details plus Auto Dispatch / Show Contractors buttons

Where notifications go

All notifications are sent to the Telegram account linked in your dashboard Settings. Only one account can receive notifications at a time.

Changing your notification account

  1. Find your Chat ID: Open Telegram → search for @userinfobot → send /start → it replies with your Chat ID number
  2. Go to Settings on your dashboard → Telegram
  3. Enter the new Chat ID and click Save
  4. A 6-digit verification code is sent to the new Telegram account
  5. Enter the code on the dashboard and click Confirm

Inbox badge

The sidebar shows an unread count badge on the Inbox icon. It updates every 15 seconds. Resolved and closed tickets are automatically excluded from the count.

How tenants send photos

Tenants can send photos via Telegram or WhatsApp. When a photo is received:

Viewing photos

13. Voice Agent (Phone Calls)

When a tenant calls +1 438-900-9998, an AI voice assistant answers the call.

What the voice agent does

  1. Identifies the caller by their phone number and greets them by name
  2. Asks what they're calling about
  3. Asks 1-2 clarifying questions to understand the issue
  4. Logs a maintenance ticket with the full details
  5. Confirms the request has been logged and hangs up

Call recordings are available on the dashboard. When you open a phone ticket, you see an audio player instead of a text conversation.

Available 24/7. The voice agent answers calls at any time of day or night. Tenants do not need to leave a voicemail.

Unregistered callers: If the caller's phone number is not in the system, the voice agent will politely ask them to contact their property manager. No ticket is created.

14. What the Bot Does NOT Do

  • It does not call emergency services or tell tenants to evacuate — your safety decisions are yours alone
  • It does not promise timelines (e.g. "someone will be there tomorrow")
  • It does not commit to sending contractors without your explicit approval
  • It does not reply to people whose phone or email isn't registered in the system
  • It does not share your personal information or contact details with tenants
  • If the AI can't handle something, it logs the ticket and you take over manually

Emergency protocol

If a tenant confirms real danger (flames, gas leak, flooding), the bot:

  • Tells the tenant their safety is the priority and the team is being notified
  • Sends you an urgent notification with dispatch buttons
  • Never tells the tenant to leave or vacate — that decision is yours

Self-help first

The bot suggests tenants try simple fixes before dispatching a vendor (e.g. plunger for a clogged toilet, checking the breaker panel for electrical issues). This saves you money and reduces unnecessary service calls.

15. Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseSolution
Tenant messages but gets no reply Phone/email not in the system, or wrong format Check the tenant's phone number on the dashboard. Must include country code (e.g. 15145551234).
Tenant not recognized on phone Caller's number doesn't match any tenant record Add the tenant with the correct phone number including country code.
No Telegram notifications Chat ID not set or not verified Go to Settings → Telegram → re-enter and verify your Chat ID.
Bot not responding after pausing Bot was paused on a resolved ticket The bot only responds on open tickets. If the ticket is resolved, the bot won't resume on it. New messages will create a new ticket.
Telegram ID column is blank Tenant hasn't messaged the bot yet Ask the tenant to send any message to the Telegram bot. The ID captures automatically.
WhatsApp messages not showing in ticket Message linked to wrong ticket Contact support — this is a technical issue that can be fixed.
Dashboard not loading Server may be restarting Wait 1-2 minutes and try again. If it persists, email support.
