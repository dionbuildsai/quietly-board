How It Works

  1. A tenant reaches out via any channel — Telegram, WhatsApp, SMS, Email, or Phone
  2. The AI bot responds automatically and asks clarifying questions
  3. A ticket is created on your dashboard with a full summary
  4. You receive a Telegram notification with the ticket details
  5. If the issue is urgent, you get dispatch buttons to contact a vendor immediately

Ticket Detail View

When you click on a ticket, you see:

  • Left panel: Ticket details — tenant, property, category, summary, attachments
  • Right panel: Full conversation between the tenant and the bot, including photos
  • Top right: Bot toggle, mark resolved, and delete buttons

Common Actions

Pause the Bot & Reply Manually

  1. Open a ticket and click Bot OFF (top right)
  2. A text input appears at the bottom — type your message and press Send
  3. Your message is sent directly to the tenant via their original channel
  4. When you leave the ticket page, the bot automatically resumes

Close a Ticket

Open a ticket → click Mark Resolved. The bot stops responding to that conversation thread.

Delete a Ticket

Open a ticket → click Delete → confirm. This permanently removes the ticket, all messages, photos, and files.

Delete a Message

Hover over any message in the conversation → click the red X → confirm.

Important: Deleting a ticket or message cannot be undone. Make sure you no longer need the information before deleting.

Telegram Notifications

You receive Telegram notifications for:

  • Every new ticket created (with category, tenant name, and summary)
  • Urgent tickets (with buttons to dispatch a vendor or view contractors)

Change Your Notification Account

  1. Go to Settings → Telegram
  2. Enter a new Owner Chat ID in the field
  3. Click Save — a 6-digit verification code is sent to the new Telegram account
  4. Enter the code on the dashboard and click Confirm
  5. Notifications now go to the new account

How to find your Chat ID: Open Telegram → search for @userinfobot → send /start → it replies with your Chat ID number.

What the Bot Does NOT Do

  • It does not call emergency services or tell tenants to evacuate
  • It does not promise timelines or commit to sending contractors without your permission
  • It does not reply to people whose phone or email isn't in the system
  • If the AI can't handle something, it logs the ticket — you take over manually

Getting Help

If the bot stops replying to tenants:

1. Open your dashboard — if it loads normally, the issue is likely temporary

2. Email hello@quietly.systems with what happened

3. You can always message tenants manually in the meantime (pause bot → reply)
